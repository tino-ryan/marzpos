Inventory Management and Supplier Interactions

SOP Codes: SOP-INV-001 to SOP-INV-004
Version: 1.0
last update: 05 January 2026
Next Review: 05 January 2027
Prepared by: Tinotenda Gozho

Quick Navigation​

  • Ordering Stock & Supplier Interactions (SOP-INV-001)
  • Receiving & Recording Stock (SOP-INV-002)
  • Stock Adjustments & Transfers (SOP-INV-003)
  • Performing Stock Takes (SOP-INV-004)

1. Purpose​

To maintain accurate inventory records, ensure optimal stock levels, prevent stockouts/overstocking, minimise losses, support efficient supplier relationships, and comply with regulations for age-restricted vape products.

2. Scope​

Who: Store Manager, Store Owner, Staff (Cashiers)
When: Ongoing (ordering/receiving), Weekly (stock takes), As needed (adjustments)
Where: Stockroom, Sales Floor, Office

3. Responsibilities​

RoleKey Responsibilities
Store ManagerMonitor levels, place orders, manage suppliers, receive/verify stock, adjustments, lead stock takes, analyse trends
Store OwnerOversee process, attend weekly stock takes, approve major deductions, handle significant discrepancies
Staff (Cashiers)Assist receiving, report damages/shortages, participate in stock takes

4. Key Definitions​

  • GRV — Goods Received Voucher (recording incoming stock)
  • Minimum Stock Level — Quantity threshold in Ultimate POS that triggers low-stock alert
  • Stock Adjustment — Correction to inventory due to damage, loss, theft, or errors
  • Stock Take — Physical count to verify system records

5. Required Tools & Systems​

  • Ultimate POS (primary system for all inventory functions)
  • Supplier websites/portals, email, phone
  • Spreadsheet software (for bulk product imports if required)
  • Physical counting tools (scales, counters)

6. Procedures​

Ordering Stock & Supplier Interactions (SOP-INV-001)​

  1. Monitor Stock Levels

    • Review sales reports and trends
    • Use low-stock alerts in Ultimate POS
    • Prioritise fast-moving & premium products

    System: Reports → Product Sell Report / Stock Reports
    Products → Edit → Set “Alert Quantity”

    Critical: Never allow popular/premium items to reach zero stock.

  2. Place Orders

    • Contact supplier via preferred method (phone, email, portal)
    • Provide complete details when adding/updating suppliers
    • Record order confirmation

    System: Contacts → Suppliers → Add/Edit
    Purchases → Add Purchase (optional purchase order)

    Critical: Verify supplier compliance with regulations. Document all communications.

Receiving & Recording Stock (SOP-INV-002)​

Steps:

  • Inspect delivery before signing
  • Verify quantity, quality, product type against invoice
  • Note discrepancies/damages immediately
  • Record receipt in system (updates stock automatically)
  • Add new products during receiving if required

System:

  • Purchases → Add Purchase → Record received
  • Bulk new products: Products → Import (spreadsheet)

Critical:
Check expiry dates (nicotine products) and damages. Do not accept incorrect/short deliveries without notation.

Stock Adjustments & Transfers (SOP-INV-003)​

Purpose: Correct inventory for breakage, loss, found items, or transfers between locations.

Process:

  • Document clear reason for every adjustment
  • Large/unusual adjustments require Owner approval
  • For multi-store: use stock transfer function

System:

  • Stock Adjustments → Add Stock Adjustment (increase/decrease)
  • Stock Transfers → Create transfer

Critical: All adjustments must be justified and recorded.

Performing Stock Takes (SOP-INV-004)​

Frequency: Weekly (with Owner present)

Process:

  • Begin with premium/high-value items
  • If discrepancies found in priority items → proceed to full count
  • Compare physical count to system quantities
  • Investigate and document all differences

System: Use Products tab + stock reports/verification tools → post-count adjustments

Critical: Hold staff accountable for unexplained losses. Deductions only after full investigation and Owner approval.

7. Completion Checklist​

  • Low-stock alerts checked & addressed
  • Orders placed with confirmation
  • Deliveries fully verified & recorded
  • Adjustments/transfers documented
  • Weekly stock take completed & discrepancies resolved
  • System stock matches physical count

8. Exceptions & Troubleshooting​

IssueAction RequiredEscalate To
Low stock alert1. Review trends
2. Place urgent order		Store Manager
Delivery discrepancy1. Note on invoice
2. Contact supplier
3. Partial receive if possible		Manager / Owner
Significant stock shortage (stock take)1. Recount
2. Investigate (CCTV/sales)
3. Adjust + consider accountability		Store Owner
Repeated system vs physical mismatch1. Adjust with reason
2. Report pattern for review		Store Manager

9. Compliance & Rules​

Legal Requirements

  • Accurate tracking of age-restricted nicotine products
  • Compliance with local vape/tobacco regulations (minimum age, record-keeping)

Company Policies

  • Mandatory weekly stock takes with Owner
  • Accountability for losses after investigation

Prohibited

  • Accepting unverified deliveries
  • Undocumented adjustments
  • Ignoring low-stock alerts

Consequences

  • Financial loss from stockouts/theft/overstock
  • Disciplinary action (up to termination)
  • Potential regulatory penalties

10. Records & Reports​

Records

  • Purchase Invoices/Orders — On receipt — Ultimate POS + physical file
  • Stock Adjustments — As occurred — Ultimate POS
  • Stock Take Sheets — Weekly — Digital/physical

Reports

  • Product Sell Report — Weekly/Monthly — Manager/Owner
  • Low Stock Alerts — Daily check — Manager
  • Stock Discrepancy Report — After stock take — Owner

Retention

  • Purchase Records: 7 years
  • Adjustments & Stock Takes: Minimum 3 years
